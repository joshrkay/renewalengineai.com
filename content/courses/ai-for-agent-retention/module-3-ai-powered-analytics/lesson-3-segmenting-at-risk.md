# Segmenting At-Risk Clients for Outreach

Once you have your weekly at-risk list, the next question is what to do with it. Every at-risk client is not the same. Some need a phone call from you personally. Some need a thoughtful email. Some just need to know you are there. This lesson shows you how to sort them quickly.

## The three-bucket system
At-risk clients fall cleanly into three buckets. Your job is to spend 30 seconds per client sorting them, and then follow the template for each bucket.

**Bucket 1: Save candidates.** Top 20 percent of the book, high premium increase, no recent contact. These are the ones who will walk if you don't pick up the phone. You owe these clients a live voice conversation before the renewal delivers. No email substitute.

**Bucket 2: Reassurance candidates.** Mid-tier accounts with one risk signal but a long tenure and a good relationship. These clients usually stay if they hear from you at all. A thoughtful, personalized email from your account is enough. AI drafts the email, you approve and send.

**Bucket 3: Automated candidates.** Bottom-tier accounts with low risk and low premium. Run the standard renewal sequence, don't invent a custom touch, and move on. If they lapse, the revenue cost is small and you have bigger fish to fry.

## The rule that keeps you sane
You cannot call everyone. A solo agent running a 600-policy book realistically has time for five to seven save conversations per week. Maybe ten if the week is light. Protect that budget.

Spending your save-call budget on bottom-bucket accounts is the classic mistake. The agent feels productive because they made calls, but the book still bleeds because the top accounts never got a call. Discipline matters more here than effort.

## Practical sorting workflow
Once a week, take your at-risk list and add three columns: Bucket, Action, Who. Bucket is 1, 2, or 3. Action is what specifically you are doing (call, draft email, automation). Who is whoever on your team is responsible. If you are solo, "Who" is always you or "automation."

Sort the list by bucket. Do bucket 1 first. Schedule the calls on your calendar before the day ends. If the call doesn't get scheduled in the week, it doesn't happen, and the account will probably lapse.

## Don't skip the easy wins
Bucket 2 is the sleeper bucket. It's where AI-assisted outreach earns its keep. Ten reassurance emails drafted in five minutes by an AI, personalized with real policy facts, approved and sent in ten minutes. That is a 15-minute investment that often retains three to five policies per week on a typical book.

Over a year, that is 150 to 250 retained policies. At a couple hundred dollars of commission each, the math speaks for itself.

**Do this today:** Take your top ten upcoming renewals and assign each one to a bucket. See how many are bucket 1. That is your save list for next week.
