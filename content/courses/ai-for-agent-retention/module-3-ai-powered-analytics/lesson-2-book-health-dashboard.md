# Building a Book Health Dashboard

A dashboard for a solo agent does not mean Tableau. It means one page you look at on Monday morning that tells you where to spend your time this week. This lesson shows you how to build it in under an hour.

## The only metrics that belong on it
Resist the temptation to track twenty KPIs. A working book health dashboard has five numbers on it and nothing else.

**Renewals in the next 30 days.** Count and total premium. This is your immediate workload.

**Renewals with a premium increase over 10 percent.** Count. This is your at-risk workload.

**Untouched accounts.** The number of top-20-percent clients who have not received any outreach from you in the last 60 days. This is your relationship debt.

**This week's save list.** The three to seven specific accounts you personally need to call this week. Not automate. Call.

**Lapse count, month to date.** One number. Unemotional. This is how you know if the system is working.

If you can answer those five questions in 30 seconds on Monday morning, you have a working dashboard. Everything else is decoration.

## The cheapest version
The cheapest version of this is a Google Sheet with five cells and a weekly checklist. Total build time: 20 minutes. Here is the structure.

Column A: Metric name.
Column B: This week's value.
Column C: Last week's value.
Column D: Trend arrow.
Column E: Notes or action required.

Refresh it every Monday morning by exporting from your AMS and pasting values. That is it.

## The medium version
If you want it slightly fancier, use Airtable or Notion. Both let you link the at-risk list directly to a table of contacts with the last touch date, the next touch date, and a notes field. You still refresh manually, but you get clickable rows.

## The automated version
The highest-effort version connects your AMS export to a tool like Google Apps Script or Zapier that writes the values into the sheet automatically once a week. This is nice to have but not necessary. Many of the agents running this system successfully never get past the Google Sheet version.

## What matters more than the dashboard
The dashboard is worthless without the 30-minute Monday ritual. The ritual is: open the dashboard, look at the five numbers, pick the three to seven accounts on your save list, and block time on your calendar this week to call them.

The dashboard is the tool. The ritual is the system.

**Do this today:** Create the Google Sheet with the five rows listed above. Leave the values blank for now. Next Monday you will fill them in for the first time.
