# Multichannel Cadence — Email, SMS, Voice

A sequence that lives only in email is leaving response rate on the table. Every independent agent has clients who never open email but answer texts within minutes, and clients who will pick up a call but ignore everything else. This lesson shows you how to layer channels without feeling intrusive.

## Channel rules of thumb
Think about each channel as having a different tolerance for intrusion.

**Email** is low-intrusion, low-urgency, and high-content. Use it for the long touches: the value reminder, the review offer, the formal renewal delivery. Email is where you deliver documents, explanations, and anything that needs to be searchable later.

**SMS** is high-intrusion, medium-urgency, and low-content. Use it sparingly. One good use is a short "I just sent you something important, wanted to make sure you saw it" nudge 24 hours after a renewal email goes unopened. Another is the final escalation reminder at X minus 7. Never cold-sell over SMS. Never send a long message. Three sentences maximum.

**Voice** (a phone call, or a voice note through a tool like WhatsApp Business) is the highest-touch channel and the highest response. Reserve it for your top accounts and for save conversations. The goal of voice is not to deliver information. It is to restore the relationship.

## A practical cadence
Here is what a layered cadence looks like in practice for a top-quartile policy.

Day X minus 75: Email touch.
Day X minus 60: Email with value recap.
Day X minus 45: Email with review offer.
Day X minus 44 (if X minus 45 is unopened after 24 hours): SMS nudge: "Hey {first_name}, just making sure my email from yesterday landed. Let me know if anything's changed on your end."
Day X minus 30: Email with renewal documents.
Day X minus 14: Email reminder if no response.
Day X minus 7: Personal phone call. No email this day. Voice only.

For mid-tier accounts, drop the X minus 7 voice call and keep everything else. For bottom-tier accounts, keep only the X minus 60, X minus 30, and X minus 14 emails. Match the effort to the revenue.

## Avoid the "I'm being hunted" feeling
The single biggest mistake new agents make is firing all of these touches from a marketing automation tool that makes them read like a marketing sequence. Run every message through this gut check: "If I got this from my dentist, would I think they cared about me or that I was in a funnel?" If it's the second, rewrite it before sending.

**Do this today:** Open your phone. Text the last three clients who had a renewal in the past month. Just "Hey, how did everything go with the renewal? Any questions I can answer?" Watch how many reply within an hour. That is the power of the second channel.
