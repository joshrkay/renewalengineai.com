# Measuring ROI and Iterating

A system you cannot measure is a system you cannot defend. This lesson walks through the three numbers that prove your retention work is paying off and how to iterate once you have them.

## Metric one: retention rate
The headline metric is the percentage of policies that renewed in a given window. Calculate it monthly. Take the count of policies with X-dates in the month, subtract the count that lapsed, divide by the total, multiply by 100.

Compare this month's number to the same month last year. If it's flat, the system is not yet working; check your personalization quality and touch cadence. If it's up by two or more points, you have a working system. Celebrate quietly and keep going.

## Metric two: recovered revenue
This is the number that makes the case to your business partner, your spouse, or yourself at 10 p.m. when you are tempted to stop doing the Monday ritual.

Take the difference between this month's retention rate and your baseline from Module 1. Multiply by the month's total renewal premium. That dollar number is what the system added to your agency this month.

A two-point improvement on a book with $60,000 of monthly renewal premium is $1,200 of recovered revenue this month. Annualized, that's $14,400. That is a retention system paying for itself roughly 30 times over in year one.

## Metric three: hours spent
Track how much time you personally spend on retention activities each week. Not your total work hours. Just the ritual: drafting outreach, running the dashboard, making save calls. This should be between three and six hours per week on a typical book once the system is running.

If it is more, something is inefficient. Check whether you are drafting from scratch instead of using the AI prompt. Check whether you are calling bucket 2 accounts that should get emailed. Optimize the ritual, not the sequence.

## Iteration cadence
Once a quarter, sit down for an hour with these three numbers and ask three questions.

First: what changed? A retention rate that moved needs an explanation. Did you change the cadence? Did you hire help? Did the market shift?

Second: where did the saves come from? Go back to your at-risk list from the last 90 days and check how many of the flagged accounts actually retained. That is your save rate. If it's above 50 percent, the system is very strong. Below 30 percent, the sorting or the messaging is off.

Third: what is the next bottleneck? Is it the time you spend on drafts? The quality of your voice samples? The volume of accounts in bucket 1? Pick one bottleneck per quarter and improve it. Do not try to fix everything at once.

## The most common iteration
Nine out of ten agents find the same bottleneck in their first quarterly review: they are not actually calling the save-candidate list consistently. The weekly ritual catches the list but the calls slip off the calendar. The fix is not a new tool. The fix is protecting the call block and treating it like a client appointment.

**Do this today:** Calculate your retention rate for the current month-to-date. Write it down next to your baseline number from Module 1. That is the gap you are trying to close.
