# The Lapse Cost Equation

In the last lesson you wrote down a rough number for what lapse is costing your agency. In this lesson we tighten that number up and turn it into a decision-making tool.

## The formula
Annual lapse cost equals policy count times average annual commission per policy times your annual lapse rate. That is the simple version and it gets you in the ballpark.

Here is a worked example. Imagine an agency with 800 policies, an average annual commission of $144 per policy, and a 15 percent lapse rate. That agency is losing 800 times $144 times 0.15, which is $17,280 in recurring revenue every single year. Not once. Every year.

Now extend that over three years of compounding. A policy that lapses this year wasn't just worth $144. It was worth the $144 this year, plus $144 next year, plus $144 the year after, plus every cross-sell and referral that relationship would have produced. Real lifetime value on a lost policy is usually two to four times the first-year commission.

## Why this matters for the decisions you're about to make
The numbers are important because they set the budget for the solution. Agents often look at retention tools and think in terms of cost. "This is $200 per month, I'm not sure I can justify that." But if your lapse cost is $17,000 per year, a tool that recovers even 20 percent of that is worth $3,400 per year. The $2,400 annual cost of the tool becomes trivial.

The same logic applies to the hours you spend building the system. Five hours of setup against $3,000 of recovered revenue is the highest hourly return you will earn this year.

## Segment the number
One more cut. Break your book into three buckets by premium size. Take the top 20 percent, the middle 60 percent, and the bottom 20 percent. Your top 20 percent is almost always where the lapse cost concentrates. Losing one big commercial account often equals losing 15 small personal lines policies.

When you build your retention system in the next modules, you are going to spend most of your attention on that top bucket. The framework is still trigger-based for everyone, but the messaging depth and the human-touch moments get reserved for the accounts that move the number.

**Do this today:** Open a spreadsheet. Put your policy count in one cell, average commission in another, and lapse rate in a third. Multiply them together. That is the minimum value your retention system needs to unlock to pay for itself.
