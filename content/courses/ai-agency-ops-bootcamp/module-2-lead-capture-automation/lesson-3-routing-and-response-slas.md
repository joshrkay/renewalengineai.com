# Routing and Response SLAs

Classifying a lead is useless if nobody responds to it. This lesson is about the two mechanics that turn a classified lead into a fast, consistent response: routing (who gets the lead) and SLAs (how fast they have to act).

## The speed-to-lead reality
Industry research on lead response times is brutal. A lead contacted within five minutes is roughly nine times more likely to convert than one contacted within an hour. A lead contacted within an hour is several times more likely to convert than one contacted the next day. Speed dominates almost every other factor.

Most agencies have no SLA. Leads come in, they sit, they get handled when someone notices them. The fix is not software. The fix is a rule, enforced.

## The routing matrix
Start with a simple matrix. On one axis, the classification output from Lesson 2.2: urgency and line of business. On the other axis, your team members (or just yourself if you're solo).

A minimal matrix for a two-person agency looks like this.

High urgency, personal lines → producer (call within 15 minutes).
High urgency, commercial → owner (call within 15 minutes).
Medium urgency, any line → producer (email within 1 hour, call within 4 hours).
Low urgency, any line → CSR (email within 4 hours).

Add rows as your team grows. The principle stays the same: faster response for more urgent work, higher-skill person for the more complex work.

## Enforcing the SLA
Rules without enforcement are wishes. There are three light-touch ways to enforce response times.

**Visible timer.** When a lead arrives in your Slack or your tracking sheet, note the arrival time. A simple column or emoji can flash red after 15 minutes if not acted on.

**Escalation path.** If the assigned person has not claimed the lead in half the SLA window, the system reassigns it to a backup person and pings both.

**Weekly review.** Every Monday, pull the week's leads and their response times. Discuss any that missed the SLA. Not to blame, but to fix the system.

Any one of these works. All three is overkill. Pick one and start there.

## The automation ceiling
This is a good place to remember what AI is not doing. AI is classifying and routing. AI is not calling the lead. A human makes the call. AI can draft the opening email, but a human approves and sends.

Trying to have AI handle the full opening conversation with a prospective client is a bad idea today. Licensing, trust, and nuance all argue for a human voice on the first real conversation. Use AI to get the right lead to the right human fast. Let the human close.

## What good looks like
A few months into this system, you should see median lead response time drop from whatever-it-was to under 15 minutes for high-urgency leads. Conversion rates on inbound leads should climb noticeably. You will feel it in month one and see it in the numbers by month three.

**Do this today:** Pick one SLA number. Just one. "High urgency leads get called within 15 minutes." Commit to it for two weeks and see what breaks. That is the actual implementation.
