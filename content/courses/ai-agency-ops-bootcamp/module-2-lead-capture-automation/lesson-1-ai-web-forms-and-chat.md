# AI Web Forms and Chat

The first impression your agency makes is almost always a web form or a chat widget. Most agencies set theirs up once in 2018 and never looked at it again. This lesson shows you how to upgrade that first impression with AI without rebuilding your site.

## The problem with static forms
A traditional "Request a Quote" form asks the same ten questions every time. Name, email, phone, address, what kind of insurance, current carrier, drop-down for coverage type. The visitor fills it out, or more likely, abandons it halfway through because it feels like a chore.

Abandonment rates on static quote forms often run above 60 percent. Every abandoned form is a lost lead that will probably end up with a competitor who made the process easier.

## The AI-assisted alternative
An AI-assisted intake form starts with a simple, low-friction question like "What brought you here today?" and then adapts based on the answer. If the visitor says "I'm shopping for auto insurance," the next question is about vehicle count. If they say "I just bought a home," the next question is about closing date.

This is called a conversational form. There are three ways to build one without writing code.

**Option 1: A dedicated conversational form tool.** Typeform, Tally, and Youform support conditional logic and can be styled to match your site. Zero AI is technically required, but pairing these with an AI-powered follow-up (see Lesson 2.3) gives you the upgrade.

**Option 2: A chat widget powered by an AI agent.** Tools like Chatbase, Intercom Fin, and HubSpot's chat AI let you feed your agency's materials into a chat that answers visitor questions and captures contact info when they're ready.

**Option 3: An embedded AI tool you fully control.** Build a simple interface using a tool like Typedream or Softr that calls your chat AI with a custom prompt. Higher effort, total control.

## Which to pick
For solo or small agencies, Option 1 is the pragmatic choice. It is fast, the tools are cheap or free, and you can launch in under an hour. Upgrade to Option 2 when you are getting more than 20 inquiries per week and need real-time answers at 9 p.m. Skip Option 3 unless you have a technical partner.

## The setup checklist
Regardless of which option, every good intake flow has the same six steps.

One, a warm opening question that does not feel like a form.
Two, a branching path based on line of business.
Three, a clear estimate of how long this will take ("about two minutes").
Four, a capture point for name and contact info before asking anything sensitive.
Five, a confirmation message that tells the visitor what happens next and when.
Six, an automatic routing to your inbox with the captured data formatted cleanly.

Step six is where many agencies fall down. If the form captures data but it arrives in a disorganized email, you will not act fast enough. We fix that in Lesson 2.2.

**Do this today:** Visit your own agency website and fill out your own quote form. Time it. Most agents are shocked by how clunky their own flow feels.
