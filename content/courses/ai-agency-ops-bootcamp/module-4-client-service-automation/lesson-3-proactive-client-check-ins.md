# Proactive Client Check-Ins

Most agencies are reactive. A client calls, the agency responds. This lesson is about flipping the script with AI-powered proactive check-ins that catch problems before they turn into cancellations and create moments of delight that competitors cannot match.

## The four moments that matter
Not every check-in is worth automating. The four moments that consistently produce retention and cross-sell lift are these.

**30 days after bind.** The new client has had enough time to have questions and not enough time to have forgotten who you are. A short check-in at day 30 catches confusion early, reinforces the relationship, and often produces a quick cross-sell conversation.

**After a claim closes.** Claims are the agency's report card. A thoughtful check-in within three days of a claim closing, asking how the experience was, is the single most impactful retention moment you have.

**Policy anniversary.** Not the renewal. The one-year mark of the relationship. A short note acknowledging "it's been a year" positions you as the thoughtful option.

**Life event triggers.** New home, new baby, new business, new state, new job. When you learn a client has experienced a life event, a same-week check-in to review coverage is gold.

You do not need all four to start. Pick one, prove it, add the next.

## The check-in drafting workflow
The AI workflow for proactive check-ins is a light variation on the personalization workflow from the retention course.

Step 1: A trigger fires (30-day bind anniversary, claim closure, etc.).

Step 2: Your automation pulls the client's facts from your AMS export plus any relevant context (claim outcome, bind date, policy details).

Step 3: AI drafts a short, personal message using those facts and your voice samples.

Step 4: A human reviews, takes five seconds, and sends.

Step 5: The outcome (did the client respond, did it produce a cross-sell) gets logged for measurement.

## Don't let the check-in feel like marketing
The difference between a check-in that builds relationship and one that reads as marketing is subtle but important. Three rules.

**No CTA at the end.** No "click here to get a quote." The check-in is a check-in, not a sales pitch. If a cross-sell is appropriate, the client will raise it themselves in the reply.

**Reference something specific.** "How did the claim on your car go" or "how's the new house." Something only a human could have noticed. This is where the fact blocks in the AI prompt earn their keep.

**One question only.** End with a single open-ended question. "How are things?" or "Anything on your mind?" That invites a reply without feeling like a survey.

## Measurement
Track two numbers on your proactive check-in program. Reply rate and reply value.

Reply rate is the percentage of check-ins that get any kind of response. A healthy rate is above 25 percent.

Reply value is the number of replies that turn into a meaningful conversation: a cross-sell, a referral, a claims clarification, a relationship-deepening exchange. A healthy rate is about one in five replies.

If reply rate is low, the messages are too templated. If reply value is low, you are asking the wrong question.

**Do this today:** Identify the last five clients who had a bind 30 days ago and send each of them a personal check-in message. Do it by hand. Feel the texture of the conversation before you automate it.
