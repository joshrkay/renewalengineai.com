# The AI Landscape for Agencies

Before you build anything, you need a clear-eyed view of the tools and categories that actually matter for an insurance agency. The landscape looks confusing because there are too many vendors shouting for attention. The good news: most of them do not apply to you. This lesson cuts the noise.

## Four categories that matter
**General-purpose chat models.** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. These are your workhorses. You will use one of them for drafting, summarizing, extracting, and thinking-out-loud. Pick one and commit. Switching between them costs you more in friction than you save in capability.

**Document and OCR tools.** These extract structured data from PDFs and scanned forms. Relevant for carrier statements, declarations pages, and ACORD forms. Examples: the document features built into Claude and ChatGPT, plus dedicated tools like Docparser or Mindee.

**Workflow automation.** Zapier, Make.com, and n8n. These are the glue between your AI, your email, your AMS, and your spreadsheets. You need exactly one of these. Pick based on price and learning curve.

**Vertical AI for insurance.** A handful of startups are building AI specifically for insurance workflows. Most are not yet ready for solo or small-agency use. A few are. We'll flag the ones worth watching, but the honest answer is that general tools plus a workflow layer get you 80 percent of the value at 10 percent of the cost.

## What you can ignore
Ignore anything that requires a custom integration into your AMS unless your AMS vendor has already built it. Ignore any tool that needs your E&O coverage to be rewritten. Ignore any tool that claims to replace a licensed producer.

Also ignore, for now, everything in the "AI agents" category that promises to autonomously run your inbox or your phone. The technology is real but it is not ready for licensed-profession deployment. Wait two years on that.

## Start with the thinnest stack
The thinnest working stack for an agency is: one chat AI subscription, one workflow tool, one spreadsheet. That is it. Do not add anything else until you have those three wired together and producing value.

Agents who try to stack five tools before building one workflow always fail. Agents who build one workflow with three tools and scale from there always succeed. The discipline is knowing the difference.

## A visit to your toolbox
Open your current software stack and write down everything you pay a subscription for that touches AI or automation. Most agencies discover they already pay for two or three tools they don't fully use. Before you buy anything new, use what you have.

**Do this today:** List every subscription in your agency. Circle the ones that include AI or automation features. You probably already have half the stack you need for this course.
