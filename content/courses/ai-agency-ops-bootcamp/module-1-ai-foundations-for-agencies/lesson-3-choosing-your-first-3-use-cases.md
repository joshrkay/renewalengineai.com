# Choosing Your First 3 Use Cases

You cannot roll out ten AI workflows at once. You can roll out three. This lesson is about picking the three that produce the biggest visible wins so that your team believes in the system before the novelty wears off.

## The selection criteria
A good first use case meets four criteria.

**High recurrence.** It happens at least five times per week. If the workflow triggers only twice a month, nobody remembers how to use it and the tool gets abandoned.

**Low stakes.** The cost of an imperfect output is small. No E&O exposure, no regulatory risk, no client relationship damage if a draft needs editing.

**Visible time savings.** The person doing the task today can immediately feel how much faster it got. This is what creates internal momentum.

**Single tool.** The first three use cases should each work with just your chat AI plus one other tool. Workflows that need three tools chained together are Module 2 territory, not Module 1.

## A menu of options
Here is a starter menu. Pick three. Resist the urge to pick five.

**Renewal outreach drafting.** (From the AI for Agent Retention course. If you took that, you already have this one.) High recurrence, low stakes, visible savings.

**New client welcome emails.** Every new policy bound triggers a warm welcome. AI drafts, CSR approves, send. 20 minutes of drafting becomes 2 minutes.

**COI request drafting.** Commercial clients need Certificates of Insurance constantly. AI reads the request, drafts the response with the policy details pulled from your AMS export, human approves, send.

**Claims follow-up drafting.** AI drafts the check-in messages for clients with active claims. Human edits for empathy and specifics, sends.

**Lead intake triage.** AI reads incoming lead inquiries and classifies them by urgency and line of business so you know which ones to call first.

**Proposal language drafting.** AI writes the explanatory text in commercial proposals based on the carriers and coverages selected. Saves 30 minutes per proposal.

## The three-week test
Once you pick three, give each one three weeks of runway. Week one is setup and training. Weeks two and three are running the workflow for real. At the end of week three, measure: did it save time, did the quality hold up, did the team actually use it?

Any workflow that fails the three-week test gets dropped, not fixed. Cutting losses fast is more important than protecting sunk cost. You can always come back to a failed workflow later with more information.

## Why three and not one
One workflow is fragile. If the single workflow runs into a problem in week two, the whole "we're using AI now" narrative dies. Three workflows create resilience. One can stumble and two can still prove the point.

More than three dilutes focus. Five workflows means none of them are your top priority, which means none of them really land.

**Do this today:** Look at the menu. Circle the three that match your agency's specific volume and pain points. Write them on a sticky note and put it on your monitor. Those are your next three weeks.
