# Building Your SOP Library

Every agency has procedures in the owner's head and almost nowhere else. That worked when you had one employee. It does not work when you have three, and it definitely does not work when you want to take a real vacation. This lesson is about building an SOP library with AI as a co-author.

## Why SOPs usually fail
Most agencies have tried SOPs before. There is a shared drive somewhere with half-written documents from 2019 that nobody has updated since. The problem is not that SOPs are a bad idea. The problem is that writing them from scratch is slow and tedious, and updating them is even worse.

AI changes the economics. A task that used to take 90 minutes of sitting down and writing can now be done in ten minutes of conversation with a chat AI. That makes SOP writing feel like a small task instead of a big one, which is the difference between getting written and staying in your head.

## The AI co-author approach
The approach works like this. You pick a process. You open a chat AI. You narrate how you do the task, in whatever order things come to mind. The AI drafts a structured SOP from your narration. You read it, correct the mistakes, and save it.

Here is the prompt to paste at the start of each SOP session.

> I am going to describe an agency process to you. Listen, ask clarifying questions, and then produce a structured SOP with these sections: purpose, trigger, inputs, step-by-step procedure, expected output, quality check, and escalation path. Use numbered steps. Keep language concrete and actionable. Do not invent steps I did not describe.

Then just talk. Describe how you handle COI requests, or how you onboard a new commercial client, or how you close out a claim. The AI catches the order, asks about gaps, and returns a clean document.

## Pick ten SOPs to start
You do not need 50 SOPs. You need ten. Pick the ten that matter most, which are usually these.

New client onboarding. New policy binding. Renewal outreach sequence. Claim intake. Claim follow-up. COI request handling. Endorsement request handling. Cross-sell opportunity handling. Referral handling. Lead intake and routing.

Draft all ten in a single week with AI. Total time: about two hours for the whole library. Without AI this would take weeks.

## Storage and access
Store your SOPs somewhere searchable. Notion, Google Drive, or a simple wiki tool all work. The rule is that any team member can find any SOP in under 30 seconds. If they can't, nobody uses the library and you're back to tribal knowledge.

Also, each SOP should have an owner and a review date. Once a quarter, the owner reads the SOP and confirms it still reflects how the work actually happens. If it doesn't, they update it. This ritual is what keeps the library alive over years.

## The SOP superpower
Once you have a real SOP library, you can do something agencies usually can't: train a new team member in days instead of weeks. You hand them the library, assign them one SOP per morning, and have them shadow you through it in the afternoon. By the end of week one they are productive on four of the ten procedures. By week two, all ten.

**Do this today:** Pick the SOP that would most help someone replace you on your first day off. That is the one to draft first. Open your AI, start talking.
